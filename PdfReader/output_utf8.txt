Saint Mary's Orthodox College – SMRS Proposal v1.1 Confidential SMRS – Confidential Page 3 of 10 1. Introduction The purpose of this proposal is to design, develop, deploy, and support a centralized digital recruitment platform for Saint Mary's Orthodox College (SMOC). The Saint Mary's Recruitment System (SMRS) will streamline the entire hiring lifecycle — from job posting and application submission to candidate evaluation and final selection — replacing manual and email-based processes with a secure, efficient, and transparent system. 2. Scope of Work • Requirements gathering and system analysis • UI/UX design aligned with SMOC branding • Full system development (frontend & backend) • Database design and implementation • Secure hosting & deployment • HR Administrator and HR Officer training • One-year technical support and maintenance  3. Proposed Solution – SMRS Platform SMRS will be a secure web-based recruitment management platform accessible through the college website. Key capabilities include:  • Online job posting and publishing • Dynamic job application form with role-specific fields and position-specific questions managed by HR/Admin • Automated email notifications for HR and applicants at each status milestone • HR inbox counter for new applications (no email on Opened status) • Role-based access control (Admin and Data Entry) • Application status tracking: Opened, Submitted, Considered, Shortlisted, Rejected • HR-triggered meeting invitation emails via template (on-premises or online)  4. High-Level Architecture The system will follow a modern three-tier architecture:  • Presentation Layer – Responsive web interface with secure login and role-based access • Application Layer – Business logic, workflow management, email notification engine, template management • Data Layer – Secure relational database with backup and recovery mechanisms  Saint Mary's Orthodox College will be responsible for defining and providing the required system architecture, whether cloud-based or on-premises. This includes provisioning and management of servers, database infrastructure, hosting environment, networking requirements, SSL certificate, domain name, and SMTP for emails necessary to operate the portal.

System Modules 5.1 User Access Management • HR Admin with granting Entry level access • HR Data Entry   5.2 Admin User Capabilities • Manage Users (Admin/Data Entry) – add, disable, etc. • Manage base definitions (CRUD) for Job Roles: Administrative, Teacher, Learning Support Provider, and Academic Management • Manage base definitions (CRUD) for Levels, Subjects, Departments, and Positions per role. Teacher and Learning Support Provider roles support multiple levels and subjects. Administrative roles use a single department/position assignment. • Manage position listings: open, close, or update postings • Manage free-form questions (CRUD): create, update, and delete questions; assign them to specific positions; up to a maximum of 5 questions per position     5.3 HR Interface – Inbox & Notifications When a new application reaches Opened status, no email is sent. Instead, the HR interface displays a real-time inbox counter showing the number of new applications started (e.g. "3 new applications"). This counter updates automatically in real time without requiring any manual page refresh or user interaction. Email notifications are triggered automatically at subsequent status transitions as defined in Section 7.    5.4 Application Constraints • An applicant cannot apply for the same position more than once within a defined period (enforced via email constraint) • An applicant cannot apply for more than three different positions within a defined period (enforced via email constraint) • The restriction period (default: three months) is configurable by administrators • Applications in Opened status have a validity period of 5 days from creation date, after which the application becomes locked. HR can reactivate expired applications/tokens through the system if required.

Business Process Flow 6.1 Applicant Flow  Step 1: Visit Job Portal & Email Verification The applicant visits the public-facing job portal.  The portal displays:  o A list of available job positions (if any exist), and  o A General Application option that is always available.  The applicant selects either:  A specific listed position, or   The General Application, where they may apply for one or more roles (e.g., Teacher, Learning Support Provider, Administrative, Academic Management).  Before proceeding, the applicant enters their name and email address and receives a secure verification link. Clicking the link:  Confirms the applicant’s email address   Redirects them to start the application process  If the link has expired, the request is discarded. Once the application is created (Opened status), it remains valid for 5 days from the creation date. After this period, the application is locked. HR may reactivate it upon request. 
Saint Mary's Orthodox College – SMRS Proposal v1.1 Confidential SMRS – Confidential Page 6 of 10  Step 2: Start Application Once the email is verified:  The system creates the application record with status Opened and saves it to the database, allowing applicants to resume their application from any device  The HR inbox counter is incremented   No email notification is sent at this stage Step 3: Application Constraints Check  The system validates application limits:  Maximum one application per specific position   Maximum three different positions within the defined period  For General Applications, the selected roles are evaluated within these constraints. If limits are exceeded:  The applicant is informed and cannot proceed Step 4: Complete Application Form The applicant completes the full Job Application Form (see Section 8), which includes:  Standard required sections   Role selection (for General Application: multiple roles allowed)   An optional section containing up to five position-specific questions defined and managed by HR/Admin (assigned per position), which the applicant is required to answer Step 5: Review and Submit The applicant reviews all entered information and submits the application. Upon submission:  Application status is updated to Submitted   Automated email notifications are sent to both:  o The applicant  o HR Step 6: Await HR Decision 
Saint Mary's Orthodox College – SMRS Proposal v1.1 Confidential SMRS – Confidential Page 7 of 10 The applicant receives email updates as their application progresses through statuses:  Considered   Shortlisted   Rejected  If shortlisted:  A separate meeting invitation email is sent by HR using a configured template  6.2 HR Flow   The HR workflow manages applications from initial review through to final decision:   Step 1: Login and Inbox HR logs into the SMRS portal. The dashboard displays an inbox counter showing newly opened applications. No email is sent for this status — it is surfaced only within the HR interface.  Step 2: View and Filter Applications HR accesses the submitted applications list and can filter by role, department, or date.  Step 3: Review Application 
Saint Mary's Orthodox College – SMRS Proposal v1.1 Confidential SMRS – Confidential Page 8 of 10 HR opens each application to review personal information, qualifications, work experience, references, social activities, and the applicant’s answers to position-specific questions managed by HR/Admin.  Step 4: Mark as Considered or Rejected If the application meets the criteria, HR marks it as Considered. Automated emails are sent to both HR and the applicant. If the application does not meet criteria, HR marks it as Rejected. Automated rejection emails are sent to both parties.  Step 5: Shortlist After comparing candidates in the pool for a given position, HR shortlists qualified candidates. Automated emails are sent to both HR and the applicant. HR then manually triggers a meeting invitation email using a pre-configured template. The template supports both on-premises and online meeting formats.   7. Email Notification Rules The table below defines when automated email notifications are sent and to whom throughout the application lifecycle.  Status HR notified Applicant notified Notes Opened — — No email. HR inbox counter incremented only. Submitted Yes (auto) Yes (auto) Triggered automatically upon form submission. Considered Yes (auto) Yes (auto) Triggered automatically when HR marks as Considered. Rejected Yes (auto) Yes (auto) Triggered automatically when HR marks as Rejected. Shortlisted Yes (auto) Yes (auto) Auto email sent to both. HR then manually triggers meeting invite via template (on-prem or online).  Note: The meeting invitation email sent upon shortlisting is triggered manually by HR using a configurable template. The template supports two meeting types: on-premises and online. Both HR and the applicant receive the invitation.
